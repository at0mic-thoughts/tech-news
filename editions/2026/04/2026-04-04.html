🔍 Deep Dive: The NPM House of Cards

The Axios supply chain compromise is another brutal reminder of how fragile the NPM ecosystem is. Axios is not some obscure dependency: it's used in millions of projects, from weekend hacks to critical enterprise systems. One compromised package can have devastating downstream effects.

This isn't the first time, and it won't be the last. The reliance on a vast, interconnected web of thousands of tiny packages creates an enormous attack surface. While tools like `npm audit` help, they are reactive. The core problem is architectural: a culture of pulling in dependencies for trivial tasks creates systemic risk.

This matters because the convenience of NPM has come at the cost of security and resilience. It's time for developers to be more deliberate about their dependencies, vendor critical packages, and consider alternatives like Deno or Bun that are rethinking package management security from the ground up.

📌 Why it matters for us: We use Axios. We need to audit all projects, update to a patched version immediately, and review our dependency policies.

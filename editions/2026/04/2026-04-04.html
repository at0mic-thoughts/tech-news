🔍 Deep Dive: The End of RAG As We Know It

Mintlify's virtual filesystem approach isn't just a clever hack. It's a signal that the industry is finally admitting RAG doesn't work for complex documentation. The chunking and retrieval approach breaks semantic relationships and loses context that matters.

Virtual filesystems let AI navigate documentation like developers navigate codebases. File structures preserve relationships. Directory hierarchies maintain context. The AI can explore, backtrack, and cross-reference naturally instead of hoping the right chunk got retrieved.

This matters because documentation AI has been stuck in the "similarity search" paradigm for too long. When your docs are structured knowledge, treat them like structured knowledge. The filesystem metaphor is intuitive for both AI and developers.

📌 Why it matters for us: OpenClaw's documentation could benefit from this approach. Consider restructuring help systems to be navigation-friendly rather than search-optimized.
